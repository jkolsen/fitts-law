Fitts Law Test

This website runs a pointing device speed test, adapted from Fitts's Law and ISO standard 9241-9. It was developed by Asit Parida for the CMU HCII class 05-640: Interaction Techniques, Spring, 2019 and updated by Chaoran Chen for the CMU HCII class 05-440/640: Interaction Techniques, Spring, 2022. It was updated by Jennifer K. Olsen for use in COMP 332 at the University of San Diego.
On the next page, please enter the details of the device you are testing. Note that measuring the screen diagonal size accurately is important.
Your goal is to tap or click the blue circles as QUICKLY and as ACCURATELY as possible. For consistency, be sure to interact the same way throughout the test. If you miss, the circle will turn red and wait for you to tap correctly. Please do not to change the orientation of the device or resize the window. If you are using a mobile device, be sure that the keyboard is closed before you press the “Start Test” button.
At the end of the test, you will be able to download your results in JSON or CSV format, and display them on the screen. Also, they will be copied into the class’s Google Sheet for which please remember to sign in using your USD account. Use the "Authorize" button at the top to login, or “Sign Out” to log off.
